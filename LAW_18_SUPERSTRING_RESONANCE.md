$$ m_e \propto \frac{\hbar \omega(\phi)}{c^2}, \quad \alpha \propto f(\phi) $$

Under extreme gravitational potential $\Phi$ (e.g., near a Black Hole or Neutron Star), the coupling between 4D spacetime curvature and extra dimensions induces a shift in the moduli:
$$ \delta \phi \sim \kappa \cdot \frac{\Phi}{c^2} $$
Where $\kappa$ is a coupling constant derived from the specific compactification model.

### 2.2 The Viability Threshold
Chemical stability (and thus biology) requires that the relative change in constants remains below a critical threshold $\epsilon_{bio}$:
$$ \left| \frac{\delta m_e}{m_e} \right| < \epsilon_{bio} \approx 10^{-5} $$
If the gravitational gradient $\nabla \Phi$ exceeds a critical value, the induced shift $\delta \phi$ causes $\delta m_e$ to exceed this threshold. Atomic orbitals expand or contract unpredictably, breaking covalent bonds (C-C, C-H, O-H).

**The Law's Equation:**
