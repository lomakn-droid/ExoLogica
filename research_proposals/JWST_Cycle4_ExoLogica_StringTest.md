## 2. Observational Strategy / Стратегия наблюдений

### Target Selection (Выбор целей)
We propose observing **Cold, Polluted White Dwarfs** with hydrogen-dominated atmospheres (DA type), where molecular hydrogen ($H_2$) features are detectable.
*   **Primary Target:** **GD 362** (High gravity, known metal pollution, ideal for high-res spectroscopy).
*   **Secondary Target:** **G29-38** (Variable WD, strong gravity field).
*   **Control:** High-resolution solar spectrum (for laboratory baseline comparison).

### Instrument Configuration (Конфигурация инструментов)
*   **Instrument:** **JWST NIRSpec** (Near-Infrared Spectrograph).
*   **Mode:** **High-Resolution (G395H)** grating.
*   **Spectral Range:** $2.87 - 5.14 \, \mu\text{m}$.
*   **Rationale:** This range contains dense forests of vibrational-rotational transitions of $H_2$ and HD, which are exquisitely sensitive to changes in $\mu$. The sensitivity coefficient $K_\mu \approx 0.01-0.05$ allows detection of shifts as small as $\Delta\mu/\mu \sim 10^{-7}$.

### Expected Signal (Ожидаемый сигнал)
If Law №18 holds, spectral lines of $H_2$ in the White Dwarf atmosphere will exhibit a systematic shift relative to terrestrial laboratory values, distinct from kinematic Doppler shifts or Zeeman splitting. The shift magnitude should correlate with the surface gravitational potential:
$$ \frac{\delta\mu}{\mu} \approx \kappa \cdot \frac{GM}{Rc^2} $$
Where $\kappa$ is a coupling constant derived from String Theory compactification models.
